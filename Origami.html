Camel

Video on how to make a camel

Interesting Facts About Camel

Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Because of thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.

Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.

Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.

Host chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Pigeon

Video on how to make a pigeon

Interesting Facts About Pigeon

Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.

Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies thanhumansare able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

Pigeons are actually very clean animals and there is very little evidence to suggest that they are significant transmitters of disease.

Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years. The first recordings of this date back to Mesopotamis, modern Iraq, in 3000bc.

Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.

The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.

Interesting Facts About Panda

Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.

Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).

Pandas have so many fans because they look cute.

Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.

An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Interesting Facts About Flying-Cicada

The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.

Most have red-orange eyes.

Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs.
